# 02 — Cost Recovery (Depreciation)

## What Is Depreciation?

Depreciation allows businesses to deduct the cost of tangible property over its **useful life**, rather than all at once. It's how the tax code accounts for the wear and tear on assets used to produce income.

✅ Imagine you buy a $50,000 delivery truck for your business. It won't last forever — it'll wear out over time. Rather than deducting $50,000 in year one, the IRS lets you spread the deduction over several years (the truck's "recovery period"). Each year you get a piece of the deduction as a cost recovery.

**What cannot be depreciated:**
- Land (never depreciates)
- Inventory
- Investments held for sale
- Assets not used in a trade or business

---

## MACRS — Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System

**MACRS** is the method used for all tangible depreciable property placed in service after 1986 in the United States.

### Two MACRS Systems

| System | Use Case | Depreciation Method |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| **GDS** (General Depreciation System) | Default for most property | 200% declining balance (personal prop) / SL (real prop) |
| **ADS** (Alternative Depreciation System) | Required in certain cases | Straight-line over longer recovery periods |

**When ADS is required:**
- Listed property used 50% or less for business
- Property used predominantly outside the US
- Property used for tax-exempt use
- Property financed with tax-exempt bonds
- Certain other situations

---

## 🧠 Mnemonic: "HALF-QUARTER-MONTH" — Convention Rules

> **Personal property** gets **Half**-year or mid-**Quarter** convention  
> **Real property** gets **Mid**-**Month** convention  

---

## MACRS Recovery Periods (GDS)

### Personal Property Classes

| Recovery Period | Examples of Property |
|----------------|---------------------|
| **3-year** | Tractor units for over-the-road use, horses (> 12 years old when placed in service), special tools |
| **5-year** | Computers, autos, light trucks, office machinery, research equipment |
| **7-year** | Office furniture and fixtures, equipment not in other class — **this is the DEFAULT CLASS** |
| **10-year** | Vessels, tugs, water transportation equipment |
| **15-year** | Land improvements (sidewalks, fences, roads), retail motor fuel outlets |
| **20-year** | Farm buildings, municipal sewers |

### Real Property Classes (GDS)

| Recovery Period | Property Type |
|----------------|--------------|
| **27.5 years** | **Residential rental property** (apartments, rental houses) — straight-line |
| **39 years** | **Nonresidential real property** (commercial buildings, offices) — straight-line |

✅ Easy way to remember: **27.5 = Residential** (27.5 sounds like it could be a house number); **39 = Nonresidential** (commercial property lives a little longer in tax-land). Both use straight-line — no accelerated depreciation for real estate.

---

## Depreciation Methods Under GDS

| Property Type | Method | Result |
|--------------|--------|--------|
| 3/5/7/10-year personal property | **200% declining balance** switching to straight-line | Front-loaded deductions |
| 15/20-year personal property | **150% declining balance** switching to straight-line | Moderately front-loaded |
| Real property (27.5 / 39-year) | **Straight-line** | Equal deductions each year |

---

## MACRS Conventions

### Half-Year Convention (Default for Personal Property)

✅ The IRS pretends that **all personal property is placed in service on July 1** — the middle of the year. So you only get a half-year of depreciation in the first year, regardless of when you actually bought the asset.

- Personal property placed in service OR disposed of in any month → treated as placed in service/disposed of on July 1
- Results in a **half-year** deduction in year 1

### Mid-Quarter Convention (40% Test for Personal Property)

**Triggered when:** More than **40%** of all personal property placed in service during the year is placed in service in the **fourth quarter** (October–December).

✅ If you go on a shopping spree in December and buy more than 40% of your year's equipment, the IRS says: "We know you're trying to front-load deductions. Instead of giving everyone the same half-year rate, we'll calculate each quarter separately."

| Quarter Placed in Service | Mid-Quarter Fraction |
|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Q1 (Jan–Mar) | 10.5/12 = 87.5% |
| Q2 (Apr–Jun) | 7.5/12 = 62.5% |
| Q3 (Jul–Sep) | 4.5/12 = 37.5% |
| Q4 (Oct–Dec) | 1.5/12 = 12.5% |

### Mid-Month Convention (Real Property)

- **Always applies** to residential and nonresidential real property
- Property is treated as placed in service or disposed on the **15th of the month**
- First year deduction depends on the month placed in service

## Section 179 Expensing Election

**What it does:** Allows immediate deduction of the entire cost of qualifying property in the year placed in service.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2024 Limit** | **$1,160,000** |
| **Phase-out threshold** | **$2,890,000** |
| **Phase-out rate** | Dollar-for-dollar over $2,890,000 |
| **Cannot exceed** | Taxable income from active trade or business |
| **Carryforward** | Excess Section 179 carries forward indefinitely |

✅ Section 179 is like saying "I want to deduct my whole $50,000 machine right now instead of over 7 years." You can, as long as you don't spend more than ~$2.9 million on equipment in the year.

**Property that qualifies:**
- Tangible personal property (equipment, machinery, computers)
- Off-the-shelf computer software
- Qualified improvement property
- Some listed property (if business use > 50%)

**Property that does NOT qualify for Section 179:**
- Land and buildings generally
- Property used for furnishing lodging (except hotels)
- Air conditioning / heating units
- Property used outside the US

---

## Bonus Depreciation (100% Expensing — Phase Down)

| Tax Year | Bonus Depreciation Rate |
|----------|------------------------|
| 2022 and earlier | 100% |
| 2023 | 80% |
| 2024 | **60%** |
| 2025 | 40% |
| 2026 | 20% |
| 2027+ | 0% (expires under TCJA) |

**Key differences from Section 179:**
- Bonus depreciation is NOT limited to taxable income (can create or increase a loss)
- No dollar cap
- Applies to **used property** too (if not previously used by taxpayer)
- Applies to qualified improvement property (15-year MACRS)
- No election required to take it; must elect OUT if you don't want it

---

## Listed Property

**Listed property** = property that tends to be used for personal purposes and requires extra tracking:
- Passenger automobiles
- Any property used for transportation
- Computers (if not used exclusively at business location)
- Cellular phones
- Entertainment/recreation property

### Listed Property Rules

| Business Use % | Treatment |
|----------------|----------|
| > 50% business | MACRS GDS (accelerated) + Section 179 allowed |
| ≤ 50% business | MACRS **ADS** only (straight-line, no accelerated, no 179) |

**Luxury auto limits (2024 — first-year example):**
- Year 1: $12,400 (with bonus) or $12,400 without
- These caps apply after calculating regular MACRS or Section 179

> Section 179 lets you skip the wait and take it **all right now**.  
> Just don't exceed the **$1.16 million** limit or the $2.89 million investment cap.

## Exam Tips

> **Tip 1:** The **40% test** for mid-quarter convention is triggered when more than 40% of the year's depreciable personal property is placed in service in Q4. This is a commonly tested calculation step.

> **Tip 2:** Land is **never depreciable** — a classic trap. If a question gives you a building + land purchase, separate them before computing depreciation.

> **Tip 3:** **Section 179 cannot create or increase a loss** — it's limited to taxable income from active business. Bonus depreciation CAN create a loss. Know this distinction.

> **Tip 4:** The recovery period for **residential rental** is **27.5 years**; for **nonresidential** it's **39 years**. Both use straight-line and mid-month convention.

> **Tip 5:** **ADS** is required when business use of listed property drops to 50% or less. Switching from GDS to ADS also triggers recapture of excess depreciation.

> **Tip 6:** Remember bonus depreciation is **phasing down** — it was 100% through 2022, 80% in 2023, and 60% in 2024. Exam questions will specify the year.
