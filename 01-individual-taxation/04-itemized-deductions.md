# 04 — Itemized Deductions

## Standard Deduction vs. Itemized Deductions

Every taxpayer gets to choose: take the **standard deduction** (a flat amount based on filing status) OR add up your **itemized deductions** (Schedule A). You always pick whichever is **greater**.

✅ The government says: "We'll give everyone a free coupon worth a certain amount. But if you have lots of deductible expenses (doctor bills, mortgage interest, donations), you can turn in your receipts and maybe get a bigger discount." You compare the two and choose the bigger one.

### 2024 Standard Deduction Amounts

| Filing Status | Standard Deduction |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Single | $14,600 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $29,200 |
| Married Filing Separately | $14,600 |
| Head of Household | $21,900 |
| Over 65 or blind (add per person) | +$1,550 (single) / +$1,300 (MFJ) |

**When you must itemize:** A married person filing separately must itemize if their spouse itemizes (and vice versa).

**When you cannot itemize:** If you are claimed as a dependent, your standard deduction is limited.

## 1. Medical and Dental Expenses

**Rule:** Deductible only to the extent they exceed **7.5% of AGI** (the "floor").

✅ If your AGI is $100,000, you don't get to deduct your first $7,500 of medical bills. Only the amount above that floor counts.

**What qualifies:**
- Doctor, dentist, hospital fees
- Prescription drugs
- Medical equipment (wheelchairs, hearing aids, eyeglasses)
- Health insurance premiums (if not employer-provided or SE deducted)
- Long-term care insurance premiums (subject to age limits)
- Medical mileage (21 cents/mile in 2024)

**What does NOT qualify:**
- Cosmetic surgery (unless required to correct deformity from disease/accident)
- Over-the-counter drugs (unless prescribed)
- Health club dues
- Nonprescription vitamins/supplements

**Formula:**
```
Total Medical Expenses
- 7.5% × AGI
= Deductible Amount (if positive; zero if negative)
```

## 2. State and Local Taxes (SALT) — $10,000 Cap

**Types of taxes deductible:**
- State and local income taxes **OR** state sales taxes (choose one)
- Real estate/property taxes on personal residence (and other real property)
- Personal property taxes (if ad valorem — based on value)

**The $10,000 Cap (TCJA):** The total of all SALT deductions is capped at **$10,000** ($5,000 for MFS) regardless of actual taxes paid. This cap expires after 2025 under current law.

**What is NOT deductible:**
- Federal income taxes
- Estate and gift taxes
- Import duties
- Driver's license fees
- Foreign income taxes (can choose foreign tax credit instead)
- State taxes on business income — those are deducted on Schedule C/E instead

✅ Even if you paid $25,000 in state income taxes and property taxes, the IRS caps your deduction at $10,000. This cap primarily hurts taxpayers in high-tax states.

## 3. Mortgage Interest Deduction

**Acquisition debt rule:** Interest on mortgage used to **buy, build, or substantially improve** a first or second home.

| Loan Date | Deductible Principal Limit |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| On or before 12/16/2017 | Up to **$1,000,000** ($500,000 MFS) |
| After 12/16/2017 | Up to **$750,000** ($375,000 MFS) |

**Home equity loan interest:** Deductible ONLY if proceeds used to buy, build, or substantially improve the home. If used for personal expenses (car, vacation), NOT deductible.

**Points paid:** Deductible in the year paid if paid to obtain a mortgage for primary residence purchase; amortized over loan life for refinancing.

**What is NOT deductible:**
- Interest on personal loans
- Credit card interest
- Life insurance loan interest (generally)
- Investment interest expense exceeding net investment income

## 4. Charitable Contributions

**AGI Percentage Limits:**

| Type of Contribution | Donee Organization | AGI Limit |
|---------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Cash | Public charity | **60%** of AGI |
| Cash | Private foundation | 30% of AGI |
| Non-cash property (FMV) | Public charity | **30%** of AGI |
| Appreciated capital gain property | Public charity | **30%** of AGI |
| Appreciated capital gain property | Private foundation | **20%** of AGI |

✅ Cash donations are the most generous — you can donate up to 60% of your income and still deduct it all. Property donations are more restricted.

**Carryforward:** Contributions that exceed the AGI limit are carried forward for **5 years**.

**Documentation requirements:**
- Cash under $250: Receipt or bank record
- Cash $250+: Written acknowledgment from charity
- Non-cash $250–$499: Receipt from organization
- Non-cash $500–$4,999: Form 8283 (Section A)
- Non-cash $5,000+: Qualified appraisal + Form 8283 (Section B)

**What is NOT deductible:**
- Contributions to individuals
- Political contributions
- Dues to social clubs
- Value of time/services donated
- Raffle ticket purchases (unless treated as gambling loss)

## 5. Casualty and Theft Losses

**Post-TCJA rule (2018–2025):** Only losses from **federally declared disasters** are deductible.

**Calculation:**
```
Lesser of: (a) decrease in FMV, or (b) adjusted basis
- $100 floor per casualty event
- 10% of AGI floor (applied to the total of all net losses)
= Deductible Amount
```

✅ Two hurdles to jump: First, the first $100 of each disaster doesn't count. Then, you only deduct the amount above 10% of your AGI. So most casualty losses result in no deduction unless the damage is severe.

**Example:** AGI = $60,000. Car damaged in federally declared flood. Loss = $15,000.
1. $15,000 - $100 = $14,900
2. $14,900 - (10% × $60,000 = $6,000) = **$8,900 deductible**

**Theft losses:** Same rules; only if federally declared area (very rare for theft).

## The "Greater Of" Test

You itemize only when your total itemized deductions **exceed** the standard deduction. This is the bottom-line test.

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Itemized > Standard deduction | Itemize (use Schedule A) |
| Standard > Itemized | Take standard deduction |
| Equal | Either — same result |

**Strategic itemizing:** In some years, "bunching" — concentrating deductible expenses into one year — can help push itemized deductions above the standard deduction threshold.

## 🧠 Mnemonic: Medical Floor — "7.5 — The Doctor's Cover Charge"

> The IRS charges you a 7.5% cover charge before letting you into the medical deduction club. You pay your own first 7.5% of AGI in medical bills, and only the excess is deductible.

## Exam Tips

> **Tip 1:** The **$10,000 SALT cap** is a constant exam topic. Know it applies to state income OR sales taxes + property taxes combined.

> **Tip 2:** For **medical expenses**, the question will often give you AGI and total medical expenses. Compute: Total expenses - (7.5% × AGI) = deductible amount. If negative, answer is zero.

> **Tip 3:** **Mortgage interest** — the $750,000 limit applies to loans originated after 12/16/2017. Older loans still use $1,000,000. The exam may test the cutoff date.

> **Tip 4:** Remember the **60% limit for cash to public charities** — this was a temporary 100% limit during COVID years, but the permanent rule is 60%.

> **Tip 5:** **Casualty losses** post-TCJA require a **federally declared disaster**. A flood in your backyard that's not federally declared gets no deduction.

> **Tip 6:** Charitable contribution carryforward period is **5 years** — not 20 years like NOLs.
