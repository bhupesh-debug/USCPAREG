# 06 — Tax Credits

## Refundable vs. Nonrefundable Credits

✅ A **nonrefundable credit** is like a gift card — if you only owe $500 in taxes but your gift card is worth $800, you can only use $500. The extra $300 is gone. A **refundable credit** is like cash — if you owe $500 but your credit is $800, you get $300 back from the government.

| Credit Type | Can It Reduce Tax Below Zero? | Excess Creates Refund? |
|------------|------------------------------|----------------------|
| **Nonrefundable** | No | No |
| **Refundable** | Yes | Yes |
| **Partially Refundable** | Partially | Partially |

**Refundable credits:** EITC, Additional Child Tax Credit (refundable portion), AOTC (40% refundable)
**Nonrefundable credits:** Lifetime Learning Credit, Child & Dependent Care, Retirement Savings (Saver's Credit), Elderly/Disabled, Adoption Credit (generally), Clean Vehicle Credit

## 1. Child Tax Credit (CTC)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Amount** | **$2,000 per qualifying child** under age 17 |
| **Refundable portion** | Up to $1,700 as Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC) |
| **Phase-out** | Begins at $200,000 AGI (single) / $400,000 AGI (MFJ); reduces by $50 per $1,000 over threshold |
| **Qualifying child** | Under 17, US citizen, dependent, lived with taxpayer >6 months |
| **Other dependents** | $500 nonrefundable credit for qualifying relatives (non-child dependents) |

✅ You get $2,000 per kid under 17. If your taxes are already zero, you might still get up to $1,700 back in cash (the refundable portion).

---

## 2. Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

The EITC is the **largest federal tax credit program** for low-to-moderate income workers.

| # of Children | Max Credit (2024) | Phase-out begins (MFJ) |
|--------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 0 | $632 | $17,640 |
| 1 | $4,213 | $26,511 |
| 2 | $6,960 | $26,511 |
| 3+ | $7,830 | $26,511 |

**Key requirements:**
- Must have **earned income** (wages, SE income — not investment income)
- Investment income limit: **$11,600** (2024) — if investment income exceeds this, no EITC
- Must be US citizen or resident alien
- Cannot file MFS
- Cannot have qualifying child claimed by another person

✅ The EITC is fully **refundable** — if you qualify, you get it even if you owe no taxes. It's designed to reward working families.

## 3. American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Amount** | **$2,500 per student** (100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 of qualified expenses) |
| **Refundable** | **40% refundable** (up to $1,000 refundable) |
| **Eligible years** | First **4 years** of post-secondary education only |
| **At least half-time** | Student must be enrolled at least half-time |
| **Phase-out** | $80,000–$90,000 (single); $160,000–$180,000 (MFJ) |
| **Felony drug conviction** | Disqualifies the student |
| **Qualified expenses** | Tuition, fees, course materials (books, supplies) required for enrollment |

✅ The AOTC is the better education credit for most students — bigger ($2,500 vs. $2,000), and part of it is refundable. But it's only for the first 4 years of college.

---

## 4. Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Amount** | **$2,000 per return** (20% of up to $10,000 of expenses) |
| **Refundable?** | **No** — nonrefundable only |
| **Years of school** | Any year of post-secondary education; graduate school; job training |
| **Phase-out** | $80,000–$90,000 (single); $160,000–$180,000 (MFJ) |
| **Per-return limit** | $2,000 per return (not per student) |

✅ The LLC is the "evergreen" education credit — you can use it for grad school, professional development, even one class. But it maxes out at $2,000 for the whole return (not per student).

| Feature | AOTC | Lifetime Learning Credit |

## 5. Child and Dependent Care Credit

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Eligible expenses** | Care for child under 13 OR disabled dependent/spouse |
| **Max expenses** | $3,000 (one dependent); $6,000 (two or more) |
| **Credit rate** | 20%–35% depending on AGI |
| **Minimum credit rate** | 20% (for AGI over $43,000) |
| **Requirement** | Both spouses must have earned income (or be FT student) |
| **Refundable?** | No |

---

## 6. Retirement Savings Contribution Credit (Saver's Credit)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Credit for contributing to IRA, 401(k), 403(b), SIMPLE, SEP |
| **Credit rates** | 50%, 20%, or 10% of first $2,000 in contributions |
| **Max credit** | $1,000 ($2,000 MFJ) |
| **Income limits (2024)** | Single: up to ~$36,500; MFJ: up to ~$76,500 |
| **Age/student requirement** | Must be 18+, not full-time student, not dependent |
| **Refundable?** | No |

✅ This is the government's way of rewarding low-income earners who save for retirement. Make a $2,000 IRA contribution and get a $1,000 credit if you're in the lowest income tier.

---

## 7. Adoption Credit

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Amount** | Up to **$16,810 per eligible child** (2024) |
| **Special needs child** | Full credit available even if expenses are lower |
| **Phase-out** | $252,150–$292,150 MAGI (2024) |
| **Refundable?** | Generally **nonrefundable** (carryforward up to 5 years) |
| **Timing** | Expenses for foreign adoption credited in year adoption finalized |

---

## 8. Elderly/Disabled Credit

- Available to taxpayers age 65+ OR permanently and totally disabled
- Nonrefundable
- **Reduced** by nontaxable pensions and Social Security received
- Very narrow eligibility — rarely results in actual credit

---

## 9. Clean Vehicle Credit (Electric Vehicles)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Amount** | Up to **$7,500** for new clean vehicles |
| **Used vehicles** | Up to **$4,000** for used clean vehicles |
| **Income limits** | Single: $150,000; HOH: $225,000; MFJ: $300,000 MAGI |
| **Price caps** | Sedans: $55,000; SUVs/trucks/vans: $80,000 |
| **Refundable?** | No (can be transferred to dealer as of 2024) |

## 🧠 Mnemonic: "AOTC Forty, LLC Zero" — Refundability Rule

> AOTC: **40%** refundable (40 cents per dollar up to $1,000 back as refund)  
> LLC: **0%** refundable (nonrefundable, use it or lose it)

## Exam Tips

> **Tip 1:** **AOTC vs. LLC** is one of the most frequently tested education credit topics. Key differences: AOTC is per-student (up to $2,500); LLC is per-return (up to $2,000). AOTC is 40% refundable; LLC is not.

> **Tip 2:** AOTC covers **books and supplies** (required for course); LLC covers only **tuition and fees**. This distinction appears on the exam.

> **Tip 3:** You **cannot claim both AOTC and LLC** for the same student in the same year.

> **Tip 4:** For EITC, watch the **investment income trap** — if investment income exceeds $11,600 (2024), no EITC at all, even if earned income qualifies.

> **Tip 5:** The **Child Tax Credit** phases out at $200K (single) or $400K (MFJ). The phase-out rate is $50 per $1,000 of AGI above the threshold.

> **Tip 6:** Credits are **better than deductions** — a $1,000 credit reduces tax by $1,000 regardless of tax rate. A $1,000 deduction reduces tax by $220 if you're in the 22% bracket.
