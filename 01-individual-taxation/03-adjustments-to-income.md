# 03 — Adjustments to Income (Above-the-Line Deductions)

## What Are Adjustments to Income?

**Above-the-line deductions** are subtractions from gross income that reduce your income down to **Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**.

✅ Picture your total income as a big pile of money. Above-the-line deductions are the chunks you take off the TOP of that pile before you even get to figuring out your regular deductions. They're called "above the line" because they sit above the AGI line on your tax return. The leftover amount is your AGI — the most important number on your return.

**The Formula:**
```
Gross Income
- Above-the-Line Deductions (Adjustments)
= Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
- Standard Deduction OR Itemized Deductions
- QBI Deduction (if applicable)
= Taxable Income
```

---

## Why AGI Matters So Much

AGI is the **gateway number** used to compute limitations on dozens of other tax items:

| AGI-Dependent Limitation | Rule |
|--------------------------|------|
| Medical expenses | Deductible only > 7.5% of AGI |
| Charitable contributions | Limited to % of AGI |
| Passive activity losses | Phase-out starts at $100,000 AGI |
| IRA deduction phase-out | Based on AGI + retirement plan coverage |
| EITC, AOTC, LLC | Phase-outs based on AGI/MAGI |
| Social Security inclusion | Based on "combined income" (includes AGI) |
| Rental real estate $25,000 allowance | Phase-out starts at $100,000 AGI |

✅ AGI is like your "score" — the higher your AGI, the more tax benefits you lose. A lower AGI unlocks more deductions and credits. That's why above-the-line deductions are so valuable — they reduce the gateway number itself.

## All Adjustments — Detailed Reference

### 1. Educator Expenses
- **Amount:** Up to **$300** per eligible educator ($600 if both spouses are educators filing jointly)
- **Who qualifies:** K-12 teachers, instructors, counselors, principals, aides working at least 900 hours/year
- **Covers:** Unreimbursed expenses for classroom supplies, books, software, professional development
- **No AGI limitation** — available to all eligible educators regardless of income

### 2. IRA Contributions
- **Traditional IRA:** Up to **$7,000** (2024); $8,000 if age 50+
- **Deductibility phase-out:** Begins when covered by employer retirement plan
  - Single: $77,000–$87,000 MAGI
  - MFJ (covered spouse): $123,000–$143,000 MAGI
  - MFJ (non-covered spouse): $230,000–$240,000 MAGI
- **Roth IRA:** Never deductible (but grows tax-free)
- **Non-deductible traditional IRA:** Still allowed; creates basis tracked on Form 8606

### 3. Student Loan Interest
- **Amount:** Up to **$2,500** per year
- **Phase-out:** 
  - Single: $80,000–$95,000 MAGI
  - MFJ: $165,000–$195,000 MAGI
- **Requirements:** Loan must be for qualified higher education expenses; taxpayer, spouse, or dependent must be borrower
- **Not available** if filing MFS (Married Filing Separately)

### 4. Health Savings Account (HSA) Contributions
- **Requires:** Enrollment in a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
- **2024 limits:** $4,150 individual; $8,300 family; +$1,000 if age 55+
- **Triple tax benefit:** Contributions deductible, growth tax-free, withdrawals for qualified medical expenses tax-free
- **Employer HSA contributions** reduce the taxpayer's own deductible contribution limit

### 5. Moving Expenses (Military Only)
- **Post-TCJA (after 2017):** Only **active duty military** members can deduct moving expenses
- Civilians can no longer deduct moving expenses (was suspended 2018–2025)
- Must be a permanent change of station

### 6. Half of Self-Employment Tax
- Self-employed individuals pay **both** employee and employer sides of FICA
- **15.3% total** SE tax; the **employer half (7.65%)** is deductible as an adjustment
- Calculated on Schedule SE; flows to Form 1040 as adjustment

### 7. Self-Employed Health Insurance
- **100% of premiums** deductible for SE individual, spouse, dependents, and children under 26
- **Cannot exceed** the SE individual's net self-employment income
- Not available for months taxpayer was eligible for employer-sponsored health plan

### 8. Self-Employed Retirement Contributions
- SEP-IRA: Up to **25% of net SE income**, max $69,000 (2024)
- SIMPLE IRA: Up to $16,000 employee deferral + employer match (2024)
- Solo 401(k): Employee contribution up to $23,000 + employer contribution up to 25% of compensation

### 9. Early Withdrawal Penalty
- If you withdraw from a CD or savings account before maturity and pay a penalty, that **penalty amount** is deductible as an adjustment
- This is NOT about IRA penalties — those have a separate 10% additional tax, not a deduction

### 10. Alimony Paid (Pre-2019 Agreements Only)
- **Deductible** for divorce or separation agreements executed before January 1, 2019
- Must be cash payments; must cease at death of recipient; cannot be child support
- **Post-2018 agreements:** NOT deductible (symmetry with inclusion rule)

## 🧠 Mnemonic: AGI Phase-out Reminder — "MAGI Matters"

> When you see "phase-out," think **Modified AGI (MAGI)**. MAGI = AGI + certain add-backs (like excluded foreign income, student loan interest, IRA deductions). For most people, MAGI ≈ AGI.

## IRA Deductibility Quick Guide

| Situation | Deductible? |
|-----------|------------|
| No workplace retirement plan, any income | Fully deductible |
| Has workplace plan, income below phase-out | Fully deductible |
| Has workplace plan, income in phase-out range | Partially deductible |
| Has workplace plan, income above phase-out | Not deductible |
| No workplace plan, but spouse has plan (MFJ) | Phase-out $230K–$240K |
| Roth IRA | Never deductible |

## Exam Tips

> **Tip 1:** Above-the-line deductions are available whether or not you itemize. This makes them more valuable than below-the-line deductions, which only help if you itemize.

> **Tip 2:** The educator expense deduction is **only $300** — a favorite small-number trick question on the exam. Even if a teacher spent $800 on supplies, they can only deduct $300.

> **Tip 3:** **Student loan interest** cannot be deducted if filing MFS. Know this limitation.

> **Tip 4:** Post-TCJA, **moving expenses** are only for active military. Don't fall for the old civilian moving expense deduction on the exam.

> **Tip 5:** The adjustment for half of SE tax is calculated BEFORE the SE tax adjustment itself — you use **92.35%** of net SE income to compute SE tax (because you get a deduction for half), then deduct half of the resulting SE tax.

> **Tip 6:** For IRA deductibility, the keyword trigger is "covered by an employer retirement plan." If neither spouse is covered, there's no phase-out — full deduction regardless of income.
