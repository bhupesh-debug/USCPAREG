# Filing Status & Requirements

## ✅ The Big Picture

Imagine you're filling out a form to tell the government how much money you made. Before anything else, the government asks: "Who are you? Are you single? Married? Taking care of kids?" — that's your **filing status**. It determines how much tax you pay.

---

## 🧠 Mnemonic: **"S-MJ-MS-QW-HH"** — "**S**ome **M**arried **J**okes **M**ake **S**eparate **Q**ueen **W**idows **H**appy at **H**ome"

| Filing Status | ✅ What It Means |
|--------------|-------------------|
| **S**ingle | You're not married on December 31st. Simple. |
| **M**arried Filing **J**ointly (MFJ) | You and your spouse combine everything on one return. Best rates. |
| **M**arried Filing **S**eparately (MFS) | You're married but each file your own return. Usually worse rates. |
| **Q**ualifying **W**idow(er) | Your spouse died in the last 2 years, and you have a dependent child. You get joint rates. |
| **H**ead of **H**ousehold (HOH) | You're unmarried, pay more than half the household costs, and have a qualifying person living with you. Better than single rates. |

---

## ✅ End-of-Year Test

Think of it like a snapshot taken on December 31st:
- **Married on Dec 31?** → You're married for the WHOLE year
- **Divorced on Dec 31?** → You're single for the WHOLE year
- **Spouse died during the year?** → You can still file jointly for THAT year

🧠 **Memory Trick:** "December 31st is picture day — whatever you are on that day, that's you for the whole year."

---

## ✅ Who Must File?

You must file a tax return if your income is **at or above** the standard deduction amount for your filing status.

🧠 **Memory Trick:** "If you made more than your **free pass** (standard deduction), you gotta file."

---

## ✅ When to File

| Deadline | What |
|----------|------|
| **April 15** | Tax return due date AND payment due date |
| **October 15** | Extended due date (file Form 4868 by April 15) |

🧠 **Key Rule:** "Extension = more time to FILE, NOT more time to PAY"

✅ Think of it like a library book: You can ask for more time to return the book, but you still have to pay the late fee on the original date.

---

## ✅ Head of Household Deep Dive

To qualify for HOH, remember **"UMHQ"** — **U**nmarried, **M**aintain home, **H**alf the year, **Q**ualifying person.

1. **Unmarried** (or lived apart from spouse for last 6 months)
2. **Maintain** the household (pay > 50% of costs)
3. **Half** the year — qualifying person must live there for more than half the year
4. **Qualifying person** — dependent child, parent, or relative

✅ **Special rule for parents:** Your dependent parent does NOT have to live with you — you just have to pay for their home.

---

## ✅ Dependents: Qualifying Child vs. Qualifying Relative

### 🧠 Mnemonic for Qualifying Child: **"CARES"**

| Letter | Test | ✅ Explanation |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **C** | Close relative | Must be your child, sibling, or descendant of these |
| **A** | Age limit | Under 19, or under 24 if full-time student, or any age if permanently disabled |
| **R** | Residency | Lived with you for more than half the year |
| **E** | Eliminate gross income test | Don't even worry about their income — it doesn't matter for qualifying child |
| **S** | Support test | The child must NOT have paid for more than half of their OWN support |

### 🧠 Mnemonic for Qualifying Relative: **"SUPORT"** (yes, one P)

| Letter | Test | ✅ Explanation |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **S** | Support | YOU must provide more than 50% of their support |
| **U** | Under gross income limit | Their gross income must be under $4,700 (2023) |
| **P** | Precludes joint return | They can't file a joint return (with exceptions) |
| **O** | Only US/Canada/Mexico citizens | They must be a citizen or resident of these countries |
| **R** | Relative | Must be a qualifying relative OR... |
| **T** | Taxpayer lives with them | ...if not a relative, they must live with you the WHOLE year |

## ✅ Standard Deduction Quick Reference (2023)

| Filing Status | Amount |
|--------------|--------|
| Single | $13,850 |
| MFJ | $27,700 |
| MFS | $13,850 |
| HOH | $20,800 |

**Additional** standard deduction for age 65+ or blind: $1,500 (married) / $1,850 (unmarried)

---

## 🎯 Exam Tips

1. Filing status questions love to test the **end-of-year rule** — always check Dec 31st status
2. **HOH** is heavily tested — know the "more than half the year" and "more than half the cost" rules
3. **CARES vs. SUPORT** — know when to use each mnemonic
4. Remember: a child born on Dec 31st qualifies as a dependent for the ENTIRE year
5. A spouse who dies during the year can still file a joint return
