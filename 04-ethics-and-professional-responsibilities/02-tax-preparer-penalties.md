# 02 — Tax Preparer Penalties

## Who Is a "Tax Return Preparer"?

A **tax return preparer** is any person who prepares (or employs others to prepare) for compensation, all or a substantial portion of any tax return or claim for refund.

✅ If someone pays you to prepare their tax return, you are a tax return preparer in the eyes of the IRS — and you are subject to a set of civil and criminal penalties if you cross the line.

**Two types of preparers:**
| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Signing preparer** | The individual who signs the return as the paid preparer |
| **Nonsigning preparer** | Prepares part of the return but doesn't sign (e.g., associates, reviewers) |

Both signing and nonsigning preparers can be subject to Section 6694 penalties.

---

## Preparer Tax Identification Number (PTIN)

**Rule:** All paid tax return preparers must obtain and use a **PTIN (Preparer Tax Identification Number)** issued by the IRS.

- Must renew the PTIN annually
- Must use PTIN (not SSN) on returns prepared for compensation
- Failure to obtain/use PTIN: $50 per failure (up to $25,000/year)
- Intentional failure: $100 per failure (up to $50,000/year) + willful conduct penalties

## 🧠 Mnemonic: "RSM" — Authority Standards Ladder (Lowest to Highest)

> **R**easonable Basis (~20% chance of success)  
> **S**ubstantial Authority (~40% chance of success)  
> **M**ore-Likely-Than-Not (>50% chance of success)  
> The higher the standard required, the harder the position must be to support.

---

## Section 6694(a) — Understatement Due to Unreasonable Position

### The Standard

A preparer is subject to this penalty if:
1. Any part of an understatement is due to an "unreasonable position"
2. The preparer **knew or reasonably should have known** of the position

**"Unreasonable position" defined:**
- **Non-disclosed position:** The preparer did not have **substantial authority** for the position (i.e., less than ~40% likelihood of success)
- **Disclosed position (Form 8275 filed):** The preparer did not have a **reasonable basis** for the position (i.e., less than ~20% likelihood of success)
- **Tax shelter or listed transaction:** Must meet **more-likely-than-not** standard (>50%)

### Penalty Amount — Section 6694(a)

**Greater of:**
- **$1,000**, OR
- **50% of the income** derived by the preparer from preparing the return or claim

✅ If you charge $400 to prepare a return and take an unreasonable position without substantial authority, the penalty is $1,000 (because $1,000 > 50% × $400 = $200). If you charged $3,000, the penalty is $1,500 (because $1,500 > $1,000).

**Can be reduced to 20%** if the preparer demonstrates:
- Reasonable cause for the understatement and
- The preparer acted in good faith

---

## Section 6694(b) — Willful or Reckless Conduct

### The Standard

A higher penalty applies if the understatement is due to:
- **Willful attempt** to understate tax liability, OR
- **Reckless or intentional disregard** of rules or regulations

### Penalty Amount — Section 6694(b)

**Greater of:**
- **$5,000**, OR
- **75% of the income** derived by the preparer from the return

✅ The difference between 6694(a) and 6694(b) is intent. 6694(a) is "you should have known better." 6694(b) is "you did this on purpose or just didn't care." The (b) penalty is much harsher.

**No reasonable cause defense** for Section 6694(b).

---

## Section 6694 Penalties Comparison Table

| Feature | Section 6694(a) | Section 6694(b) |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Conduct | Unreasonable position | Willful/reckless |
| Minimum penalty | $1,000 | $5,000 |
| % of preparer income | 50% | 75% |
| Penalty amount | Greater of $1,000 or 50% | Greater of $5,000 or 75% |
| Reasonable cause defense? | Yes | No |
| Standard required | Substantial authority (or reasonable basis if disclosed) | N/A — willful conduct |

## The Authority Hierarchy — Detail

Tax positions must be supported by appropriate level of authority based on disclosure:

| Situation | Required Standard | Approximate Confidence |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| Undisclosed position on return | **Substantial Authority** | ~40% |
| Disclosed on Form 8275 | **Reasonable Basis** | ~20% |
| Tax shelter or listed transaction | **More-Likely-Than-Not** | >50% |

### Sources That Constitute "Substantial Authority"

- IRC and other statutory provisions
- Proposed, final, and temporary regulations
- Revenue rulings and procedures
- Tax treaties
- Court cases (weight depends on jurisdiction and level of court)
- Congressional intent (committee reports, etc.)
- IRS private letter rulings (limited weight)
- IRS information/press releases
- Notices and announcements

**NOT substantial authority:** Treatises, articles, legal opinions from practitioners (no matter how prominent)

---

## 🧠 Mnemonic: Penalty Amounts — "$1K or 50% vs. $5K or 75%"

> Section 6694(a) = **1K / 50%** (unreasonable — one offense)  
> Section 6694(b) = **5K / 75%** (willful — five times worse)  
> Notice: (b) has five times the minimum and 1.5× the percentage — it's much more serious.

## Disclosure Forms

| Form | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Form 8275** | Disclose positions that are not contrary to regulations but that may lack substantial authority. Reduces required standard from "substantial authority" to "reasonable basis." |
| **Form 8275-R** | Disclose positions that are contrary to an existing Treasury regulation. Higher threshold applies. |

**Filing Form 8275 does NOT protect against 6694(b) penalties** — willful conduct is willful regardless of disclosure.

## Aiding and Abetting Understatement of Tax

**Section 6701:** Any person who aids or assists in preparing a tax document they know will result in an understatement of tax liability is subject to:
- **$1,000 per return** (or $10,000 if the understatement relates to a corporation)
- Only one penalty per taxpayer per tax year

---

## Criminal Penalties for Preparers

| Offense | Code Section | Penalty |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Willful preparation of false return | 7206(2) | Felony; up to 3 years prison + fines |
| Fraud and false statements | 7207 | Misdemeanor; up to 1 year prison + fines |
| Conspiracy to defraud | 18 USC 371 | Up to 5 years prison |

---

## 🧠 Mnemonic: Signing/Compliance Penalties — "The Five 55s"

> Five common mechanical preparer penalties each run **$55 per failure**:  
> 1. Failure to **sign** the return  
> 2. Failure to provide a **copy** to taxpayer  
> 3. Failure to include **PTIN**  
> 4. Failure to keep **records**  
> 5. Failure to file correct **information returns**  
> Each is capped around $28,000 per year per category.

## Exam Tips

> **Tip 1:** Know the exact penalty amounts: 6694(a) = greater of **$1,000 or 50%**; 6694(b) = greater of **$5,000 or 75%**. These are directly tested on the exam.

> **Tip 2:** The standard for non-disclosed positions is **substantial authority** (~40%). The standard for disclosed positions (Form 8275) drops to **reasonable basis** (~20%). Tax shelters always require more-likely-than-not (>50%).

> **Tip 3:** Section 6694(a) has a **reasonable cause and good faith defense** that can reduce the penalty. Section 6694(b) (willful conduct) has NO defense.

> **Tip 4:** Form **8275** is for non-contrary positions needing extra protection. Form **8275-R** is for positions contrary to Treasury regulations. Know which is which.

> **Tip 5:** The "nonsigning preparer" rule: even preparers who work on only a portion of a return (e.g., a specialist who calculates depreciation schedules) can be subject to 6694 penalties if their portion contains an unreasonable position.

> **Tip 6:** Endorsing or negotiating a client's refund check is a per-check penalty of $545 — a common small-but-important rule tested on the exam.
